## THE NUMBERS THAT MATTER

### Crimes Against Women in India — NCRB 2023

India recorded **4,48,211 cases of crimes against women in 2023**.
That is **51 complaints every single hour** — while you read this document, another woman filed a complaint.

| Crime Category | Cases (2023) | % of Total | Reality |
|----------------|-------------|------------|---------|
| Cruelty by husband/relatives (498A IPC) | **1,40,000+** | 31.4% | Most unreported — true number estimated 5-10x higher |
| Kidnapping & Abduction | 86,000+ | 19.2% | Forced marriages, trafficking hidden here |
| Assault to outrage modesty | 83,700+ | 18.7% | Workplace, street, public transport |
| Rape & Sexual Assault | **32,032** | 7.1% | **85 women every single day** |
| Dowry Deaths | **6,450** | — | **17 women killed every day** over dowry |
| Acid Attacks | 200+ reported | — | Actual number 2x — police misclassify as "burns" |
| Cybercrime against women | 657 NCW complaints | — | Massive underreporting — most women don't know it's a crime |

**The iceberg problem:** NFHS-5 (2019-21) survey shows **32% of ever-married women** have experienced physical, sexual, or emotional violence. Only a fraction ever reach the police.

**Rural women bear the worst of it:** 34% of rural women report intimate partner violence vs 27% urban. Rural women are StriSakhi's primary users.

## THE JUSTICE GAP — WHY WOMEN CAN'T GET HELP

### Problem 1: Lawyers Are Unaffordable

India has approximately **1.4 million enrolled lawyers** — but a survey of 2,800 advocates found:
> **79% of High Court advocates earn less than ₹10,000 per month**

This means most lawyers are concentrated in cities, chasing the same urban clients. Rural women have almost no access.

**What a lawyer actually costs:**

| Type | Fee | What a rural woman earns |
|------|-----|--------------------------|
| District Court (basic case) | ₹15,000–50,000 | Monthly income: ₹3,000–8,000 |
| High Court appearance | ₹50,000–2,00,000 | Annual income: ₹36,000–96,000 |
| Divorce case (full) | ₹50,000–3,00,000 | 3–10 years of savings |
| Top lawyer per appearance | ₹10 lakh–20 lakh | A lifetime of earnings |

**A domestic violence victim in rural UP cannot afford even the cheapest lawyer.** The law exists on paper. Justice does not exist in practice.

### Problem 2: India Has a Severe Lawyer Deficit

Despite 1.4 million enrolled lawyers, a 2018 assessment estimated a **deficit of nearly 3 million legal professionals**, particularly in Tier-II cities and rural judicial circuits.

- **Judge-to-population ratio:** Only **21 judges per million** Indians (Law Commission recommended 50)
- **Legal aid clinics:** One clinic serves **163 villages on average**
- **Female panel lawyers:** Only **12,022** across all of India — for 700 million women

### Problem 3: Legal Aid Exists But Is Inaccessible

NALSA (National Legal Services Authority) offers free legal aid to all women. In theory. In practice:
- Women don't know it exists
- They don't know the number (15100)
- The nearest DLSA office may be hours away
- Language barrier — most legal documents are in English or complex Hindi
- Social stigma — going to a government office means the family finds out

**The free legal aid network has 41,553 panel lawyers** for a country of 700 million women. That is 1 legal aid lawyer per 16,000 women.

### Problem 4: Police Are Not Accessible Either

- Police-to-population ratio: **155 per lakh** (sanctioned: 197 per lakh)
- Rural police stations have declined between 2017–2023
- Women fear police will tell their families
- Police often refuse to register FIRs in domestic violence cases

## THE 10 CRIMES STRISAKHI ADDRESSES

### Crime 1: Domestic Violence
**Scale:** 1,40,000+ registered cases. True prevalence: **29.3% of married women** (NFHS-5).
**Law:** Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005
**Key right:** Section 17 — woman CANNOT be evicted from her home
**Why women don't know:** This law is 20 years old. Most women in rural areas have never heard of it.
**StriSakhi does:** Explains Section 17/18/19, gives 3 steps, connects to Protection Officer

### Crime 2: Dowry Harassment & Dowry Death
**Scale:** 13,479 Dowry Prohibition Act cases. **6,450 dowry deaths** — 17 per day.
**Law:** Dowry Prohibition Act 1961, IPC 498A, IPC 304B
**Key right:** Demanding dowry is a criminal offence. Giving it is also illegal.
**Why women don't know:** Dowry is normalized as "gifts." Women don't know demanding it is criminal.
**StriSakhi does:** Explains 498A, tells her husband's family can be arrested, gives 181 helpline

### Crime 3: Property & Inheritance Denial
**Scale:** Massively underreported. Most women never claim.
**Law:** Hindu Succession Act 1956, amended 2005
**Key right:** Daughters have EQUAL right to ancestral property as sons — Supreme Court 2020 (Vineeta Sharma v Rakesh Sharma)
**Why women don't know:** 65 years of culture saying "daughters have no property right." Even post-amendment.
**StriSakhi does:** Cites 2020 SC judgment, explains how to file partition suit, gives DLSA number

### Crime 4: Rape & Sexual Assault
**Scale:** **32,032 cases in 2023 — 85 women per day.** True number estimated 10x higher.
**Law:** IPC 376, Criminal Law Amendment Act 2013 (post-Nirbhaya), POCSO 2012
**Key right:** FIR must be registered. No two-finger test. Free medical examination. Compensation.
**Why women don't know:** Shame, family pressure, fear of police. Many don't know FIR is mandatory.
**StriSakhi does:** Explains rights, 1091 crisis line, One Stop Centre, compensation scheme

### Crime 5: Divorce & Maintenance
**Scale:** Hundreds of thousands of cases annually. Most women abandon claims mid-case.
**Law:** Hindu Marriage Act 1955, CrPC Section 125, Muslim Women (Protection) Act
**Key right:** CrPC 125 — maintenance WITHOUT filing for divorce. No court fee. Interim relief in 60 days.
**Why women don't know:** Women believe they must choose: stay or leave with nothing. Section 125 is unknown.
**StriSakhi does:** Explains that maintenance ≠ divorce, explains interim maintenance, gives DLSA

### Crime 6: Workplace Sexual Harassment
**Scale:** 2,217 NCW complaints. True number: most women never report.
**Law:** POSH Act 2013
**Key right:** Every employer with 10+ employees MUST have ICC. You cannot be fired for complaining.
**Why women don't know:** Most women don't know POSH Act exists. Small companies pretend ICC is not required.
**StriSakhi does:** Explains POSH Act, how to file with ICC or District Officer, what employer must do

### Crime 7: Stalking & Cybercrime
**Scale:** 657 NCW cyber complaints — a fraction of reality. India's first cyberstalking conviction was 2021.
**Law:** IPC 354D (stalking), IT Act Section 66E (privacy violation), IT Act 67 (obscene content)
**Key right:** Online harassment is a criminal offence. Police can act without physical violence.
**Why women don't know:** Women think "online" is not real crime. Police often dismiss it.
**StriSakhi does:** Explains IPC 354D, National Cyber Crime helpline 1930, how to file complaint

### Crime 8: Acid Attack
**Scale:** 200+ registered cases. West Bengal alone: 16 official, actual estimated double.
**Law:** IPC 326A (acid attack), IPC 326B (attempt), Vishaka Committee guidelines
**Key right:** Immediate free medical treatment (hospitals cannot refuse). Minimum ₹3 lakh compensation.
**Why women don't know:** Victims are in shock. Families often accept out-of-court settlements under pressure.
**StriSakhi does:** Immediate emergency response, NALSA acid attack scheme, Acid Survivors Foundation India

### Crime 9: Child Custody
**Scale:** Thousands annually after divorce cases.
**Law:** Guardians and Wards Act 1890, Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act 1956
**Key right:** Mother gets natural custody of children under 5. Best interest of child is paramount.
**Why women don't know:** Fathers threaten "I will take the children." This is often legally false.
**StriSakhi does:** Explains custody law, interim custody application, Family Court procedure

### Crime 10: Forced Marriage & Trafficking
**Scale:** Kidnapping/abduction: 86,000+ cases (19.2% of all women's crimes).
**Law:** IPC 366 (abduction for marriage), Immoral Traffic Prevention Act, Prohibition of Child Marriage Act
**Key right:** Any marriage of girl under 18 is voidable. Forced marriage at any age is criminal.
**Why women don't know:** In many communities, this is presented as "normal."
**StriSakhi does:** Emergency response, anti-trafficking helpline 1800-419-8588, CHILDLINE 1098

## THE STRISAKHI ANSWER — ONE PARAGRAPH FOR JUDGES

India has 4,48,211 women who reported crimes in 2023 — and an estimated 10x more who stayed silent. The law protects them. They just don't know it. A domestic violence survivor doesn't know Section 17 gives her the right to stay in her home. A daughter doesn't know the 2020 Supreme Court ruling gives her equal property rights. A woman being harassed at work doesn't know POSH Act mandates an Internal Complaints Committee at her company.

StriSakhi is a voice-first AI that speaks their language — literally. A woman in a village in UP speaks Hinglish into her phone. StriSakhi understands. Responds in pure Hindi Devanagari. Cites the actual section of the actual law. Gives her three steps to take today. Tells her the free helpline number. Plays it back in a warm Indian female voice, slowly, so she can understand even if she can't read well.

No internet needed. No lawyer needed. No ₹50,000 retainer. 
Just the law — in her language — in her hands.
